Issuance runs on a **branching frontier** (each active record triggers `Binomial(2, σ/2)` downstream,
mean branching ratio `σ`; `σ=1` is the absorbing-state critical point — the same object as the R6 SOC
probe). Two regimes:

- **SOC / avalanche regime** — at exact criticality (`σ=1`) the avalanche-size law is the mean-field

- **variance-limited regime** — off criticality a characteristic scale appears and independent
  counting **self-averages at ½** (Part A's floor; the DE scaling).

> **Λ := the distance-from-criticality `δ = σ − 1`** — a **relevant (RG) coupling**, set as issuance
> **initial data**, realised as the **crossover scale `N_c`** between the two regimes.

**Honest note, up front (as pre-registered).** "3/2 − ½ = Λ" is **NOT** a literal formula — exponents
are dimensionless and don't subtract to a coupling. The content graded here is **Λ = the near-critical
deviation = the crossover scale**; the two exponents merely *label* the regimes the crossover
interpolates. That content is what the probe measures.

### B.1 POSITIVE CONTROL — Λ=0 gives pure SOC and NO dark energy

Tuned exactly to criticality (`δ=0`): fitted **τ = 1.486** (mean-field SOC 3/2), and the mean progeny
**diverges with the cutoff** (293.6 → 1451.4 as the size cap grows) — **no finite crossover N_c, no
characteristic scale, NO dark energy**. Dark energy *requires* an off-critical initial condition. The
control has teeth: at Λ=0 the mechanism produces exactly the SOC 3/2 the R6 probe found, and nothing
else.

### B.2 Off-critical ⇒ the crossover law `Λ ~ 1/√N_c` is DERIVED, not fit

For a subcritical branching process (mean `m = σ = 1−δ`), standard identities give
`⟨T⟩ = 1/(1−m) = 1/δ` and `Var(T) = v/(1−m)³`, so the **power-law cutoff scale**
`N_c := ⟨T²⟩/⟨T⟩ ~ v·δ⁻²`. Measured:

| δ (= Λ) | N_c | 1/√N_c |
|---|---|---|
| 0.200 | 17.0 | 0.243 |
| 0.100 | 61.7 | 0.127 |
| 0.050 | 214 | 0.068 |
| 0.025 | 774 | 0.036 |

- `⟨T⟩ ~ δ⁻¹`: fitted **slope −0.994** (theory −1).
- `N_c ~ δ⁻²`: fitted **slope −1.844** (theory −2, the mean-field cutoff).
- **`Λ = δ` vs `1/√N_c`: slope +1.084** (target +1) ⇒ **`Λ ~ 1/√N_c`**, DERIVED from the branching-
  process criticality, **not** imposed.

So the anchor's `Λ ~ 1/√N` re-appears here in a **new, structural** guise: **N is the crossover scale
`N_c`, and Λ is the distance-from-criticality that sets it.** Λ is a **control parameter / initial
condition**, not a fundamental constant.
