# Lead: neural scaling laws ↔ Λ ~ 1/√N

Joe's cross-domain flag: neural-net training scaling laws "feel similar" to Λ ~ 1/√N. Held honestly,
there is a real bridge — a shared *mechanism family*, not a shared number.

## The three threads (why it's worth looking into)
1. **Same CLT / fluctuation origin (the LLN-residual, generalized).** In the *variance-limited*
   regime, neural test loss scales as the **statistical error of a finite sample** — `~1/√D` (std) or
   `~1/D` (variance) in dataset size `D`. That is the *same* central-limit fluctuation that makes
   `Λ ~ 1/√N` (Sorkin: Λ = the 1/√N fluctuation of the causal-set volume). So the "dark energy IS the
   LLN residual of the record count" framing is **not cosmology-specific** — it is the generic
   finite-N statistical residual, showing up in ML too. Cross-domain corroboration of the mechanism.
2. **Exponent-from-spectral-dimension = the SAME math as our DeWitt route.** In the *resolution-limited*
   regime, the neural scaling exponent is set by the **intrinsic dimension of the data manifold /
   the spectrum of the kernel** (Sharma–Kaplan `α ≈ 4/d`; Bahri/Maloney spectral treatments) — i.e.
   heat-kernel / spectral-dimension methods. That is the **same machinery as the DE-amplitude DeWitt
   route** (the (9,5)/gimmel fiber measure). So neural-scaling derivations are a candidate **toolkit
   for the O(1) coefficient** (and any correction to the ½ exponent from the effective spectral
   dimension of the causal set / the fiber).
3. **Clean power-laws from self-organizing training = candidate R6a existence-proof.** Neural nets
   produce remarkably clean power-law scaling from training dynamics with **no tuned set-point** for
   the exponent (arguably criticality/edge-of-stability). If so, that is an **existence proof that a
   set-point-free dynamics yields a native power-law exponent** — exactly what the R6a SOC swing is
   testing. A real system where "the exponent falls out, unimported."

## Honest caveats
- **Different numbers.** Neural exponents (~0.05–0.5 by regime) are not ½; the claim is *shared
  mechanism family* (finite-N statistics + spectral dimension), not identical law. Do not overclaim.
- **Contested theory.** Neural-scaling "why" is unsettled (Kaplan vs. Chinchilla; manifold-dimension
  vs. kernel-spectrum). Import as a *hypothesis/tool*, never as settled derivation (DU methods rule).
- **Analogy grade** until a concrete shared object (a specific spectral-dimension → exponent map that
  applies to BOTH the data manifold and the causal-set fiber) is built and tested.

## Recommended timing
Fire **after** the R6 wave (R6a SOC-vs-set-point, R6b Λ↔N fixed-point) lands, so it can be pointed at
the *exact* remaining gap — most likely the **O(1) coefficient** in `Λ = c/√N` — using the spectral-
dimension toolkit, and cross-checked against whether R6a already found the native exponent. If R6a
finds the ½ set-point-free, thread 3 becomes corroboration; if not, neural scaling may show the route.
