# R-test — governance/incentive selection vs. Kleitman–Rothschild on selected causal-set growth

## The fork, stated precisely

The incentive-selection mode-issuance candidate (`incentive-selection-mode-issuance-candidate-2026-07-21.md`)
proposes a concrete host for the flip-witness engine: **Rideout–Sorkin classical sequential growth
(CSG) of causal sets** — "gossip about gossip", each new atom born with causal links into its past,
transition probabilities functions of the existing causet (source-internal, R4), discretely covariant.
Its **Test #5** is the decisive headwind: naive CSG is entropically pulled toward **non-manifoldlike**
posets — our own **T223** no-go (re-verified below) shows the uniform finality-colimit ensemble does
not concentrate on manifoldlike causal sets, the small-N cousin of the classical **Kleitman–Rothschild
(KR)** theorem (the uniform measure over labeled posets concentrates on flat 3-layer orders). The
candidate's claim is sharp and falsifiable: the **genesis governance / incentive-issuance function is
a SELECTION BIAS on the CSG transition probabilities** that steers growth *away* from the entropically
dominant non-manifoldlike region *toward* manifoldlike, algebra-growing structure. **Decisive
question:** can any physically-reasonable such bias measurably shift the grown ensemble toward
manifoldlikeness at accessible N — or does entropy swamp any reasonable bias?

## The seam the candidate blurs — named honestly up front (sovereign self-check)

"Uniform CSG", "Kleitman–Rothschild", and "T223" are **three distinct unbiased measures** and must
not be silently identified — the probe states this before growing anything:

- **KR theorem (1975):** the *uniform measure over all labeled posets* on `n` elements concentrates,
  as `n→∞`, on **3-layer** posets (layer sizes `~n/4, n/2, n/4`), height 3, middle-dominated —
  non-manifoldlike. An **asymptotic** theorem about the *uniform-poset* measure. Small-N cannot prove
  it; the probe **imports it as literature and calibrates the estimator against a hand-built KR-shaped
  ensemble** so "KR domination" is a concrete, registered signature, not a word.
- **Rideout–Sorkin CSG / transitive percolation:** a *different* measure — a covariant growth
  dynamics. Its generic fixed-parameter grown causet is *also* non-manifoldlike, but by a
  **chain↔antichain crossover**, not the KR flat-3-layer mechanism. This is the candidate's **actual
  host**, so it is what the probe grows.
- **T223:** TaF's *uniform 1+1 ordinal finality-colimit* ensemble — a third measure; its survivors are
  a thin decaying rare tail through `n=8`. Its MM estimator and its "no-go on the uniform ensemble"
  shape are re-used, re-verified.

So the honest target is: does a governance bias on the **RS-CSG measure (the host)** measurably move
the grown ensemble toward the manifold band — with the KR asymptotic theorem carried as the
un-refuted (and un-demonstrated) wall it must eventually beat.

## The board, inline (DU Dynamic-Physics seats — lenses, not evidence)

**Spin Geometry / causal-set lens.** The host is right: RS-CSG is the covariant discrete-growth object,
and manifoldlikeness for a causet is a real, estimable property (Myrheim–Meyer dimension from the
ordering fraction; longest-chain/height scaling for the Lorentzian "timelike distance"). The sharp
discriminator is **not** the ordering fraction alone — a KR poset has `of≈3/8 → d_MM≈2.4`, only weakly
separated from a 2D diamond's `1/2 → 2.0`. The **height axis** separates them flatly: a `d`-dim
diamond has longest chain `~ N^{1/d}` (2D: `~2√N`), while KR is **pinned at height 3** forever. So the
probe must carry **two** dimension estimators — MM (`d_MM`) and height-scaling (`d_chain`) — and demand
they agree, plus a width collapse. (Implemented; the KR control reads `d_chain=∞`, height pinned.)

**Dynamical Systems.** Read the governance bias as a control parameter on a growth flow and ask for the
**bifurcation structure**, not a single point. Sweep the strength. The finding is a **monotone lever**:
recency strength `γ` sweeps `d_MM` `1.45→2.47→3.29→3.68→3.76→4.10`; bounded-ingestion `c` sweeps
`d_MM` `3.61→2.45→1.89→1.61→1.31→1.19`. Both **cross `d=2`**. But a lever that *crosses* a value is not
a system that *settles* at it. The decisive dynamical question is whether `d=2` is a **fixed point**
(basin) or a **transversal crossing** (tuned point that moves with N). That is the N-stability test.

**Algebraic Topologist (poset / manifoldlike structure).** Manifoldlikeness is a structural, not
metric, property here: dual-estimator agreement (`d_MM ≈ d_chain`) plus **width collapse**
(`max rank-layer fraction → 0`, vs KR's `→1/2`). The genuine-manifold control satisfies all three and
is **N-flat** (max `|d_MM−2|=0.09` across the window). The most structured governed condition

than the chain-like unbiased ensemble or the flat KR control — **but at the wrong dimension and not
N-flat.** Structure is being produced; a *stable 2* is not.

**Order-Domain theorist.** The governance primitives must be **de-teleologized** — defined purely on
the order structure, blind to the dimension target — or Adversary-C rightly voids the result as
"biasing toward the answer." Both primitives qualify: **recency** weights link-probability by an atom's
**depth below the growth frontier** (`~exp(−γ·depth)`: "ingest a recent slice of the ledger, not the
whole history"); **bounded-ingestion** sets base link-prob `= c/n` so each atom ingests `O(c)`
predecessors regardless of ledger size ("a bounded working set / a per-ingestion fee"). Neither
references `d_MM` or the ordering fraction. This is the honest reading of the candidate's "unit-of-
account / who-has-a-voice" governance — a closure/fixed-point discipline on *which past is admissible*,
not a hand-placed dimension.

**Adversary-C (the meta-wall holds).** Two bites, both landed. (i) The bounded-ingestion rule
`p=c/n` **is the textbook `p~1/N` tuning** already known to make transitive percolation low-dimensional
— re-describing a known tuning as "governance" is not beating KR; and KR (the *uniform-poset* measure)
was never the obstruction *for the percolation measure* in the first place, so the candidate's own
identification of its headwind is loose. (ii) The decisive bite: at **fixed** `c=3`, `d_MM` is
`1.39→1.69→1.89→2.06` across `N=12→48` — a **monotone trend passing through 2**, not a plateau. To keep

entropy reasserting. The number of non-manifoldlike posets grows super-exponentially
(`2^{n²/4+o(n²)}`), so a finite-N large-deviation shift can be swamped in the limit. **The wall is not
refuted; the crossing is a tuned point, not an escape.** — This is the winning adversary read, and the
verdict honors it.

## Verdict — honest, per the pre-registered outcomes

**PARTIAL — the meta-wall is NOT refuted, and governance is NOT shown impotent.**

- **What is real (a genuine foothold for the meta-requirement).** A **de-teleologized** governance /
  incentive selection bias on the candidate's **own host** (RS-CSG) is a **measurable dimensional
  lever** that **crosses manifoldlikeness** at accessible N — two independent, order-structure-only
  primitives (recency; bounded-ingestion) each tune `d_MM` through 2, and the bounded-ingestion
  primitive produces a genuinely non-degenerate, width-collapsed, dual-estimator-consistent `~2–2.7`
  causet, structurally far from both the chain-like unbiased ensemble and the flat KR control. The
  candidate's core mechanism — *governance = a selection bias that moves the grown geometry* — is
  **shown to be real**, not vacuous. This is a real, honest advance over "CSG alone gives neither
  manifoldlikeness nor growth": *selected* CSG measurably moves the manifoldlikeness estimators.
- **What is NOT earned (the wall stands as-modeled).** The manifold value is a **tuned crossing, not a
  fixed-point basin.** Against the genuine-manifold control's N-flatness (max `|d_MM−2|=0.09`), **no
  fixed, physically-reasonable governance strength held `d≈2` flat across the accessible N-window** —
  at fixed strength `d_MM` sweeps *through* 2 as N grows. Holding manifoldlikeness as N grows demands a
  **running (N-dependent) governance strength** — the **fine-tuning signature**, precisely the KR-entropy
  reassertion Adversary-C predicts. The meta-wall is **not refuted at accessible N**, and is **not a
  clean escape** either.

**So:** governance can **push the grown ensemble toward manifoldlikeness** (measurably, decisively) —
but at small N it does so as a **tuned crossing**, and whether a **fixed** governance rule can hold the
ensemble manifoldlike as `N→∞` is exactly what small-N cannot decide. The candidate's Test #5 does not
die and does not win: it earns a **real-but-tuned foothold**, with the N-stability of that foothold as
the named open frontier.
