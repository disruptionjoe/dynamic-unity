# The records/finality account of the double-slit

Prompted by the question: is there a double-slit-style experiment that could *test* the
records/finality account of measurement — the intuition that "the particle knows it will be
detected because the metastable set of causal events has already recorded the deterministic part
in the global set"? Findings, at honest grade.

## 1. There is no retrocausality to "save"
In the delayed-choice / quantum-eraser setup, **no-signaling** guarantees the marginal pattern on
the screen is *always* the no-interference blob, regardless of any downstream choice. Interference
reappears only under **post-selection** (correlation with the idler). So "the particle knew it would
be detected" is not a phenomenon needing a mechanism. What is real is **correlation-determinism**:
the signal and idler are perfectly correlated, and that correlation is rigid. QM *gives* the
correlation but leaves *why it is rigid* as brute fact.

## 2. The records account EXPLAINS that rigidity — but as an interpretation, not a prediction
"The metastable set of causal events has already recorded the deterministic part in the global set"
is, in DU's vocabulary, TI's **H¹ global section of finalized bindings**: one finalized record, and
both outcomes are *readouts* of it — which is why it "feels super deterministic." This is a genuine
**explanatory reframe** (relocation grade — consistent with Bet #1's two right-type relocations). But
it is **block-universe-compatible**, so as an interpretation it predicts *identically* to QM. An
interpretation has no discriminating experiment. **The vanilla double-slit — even delayed-choice —
cannot distinguish this account from textbook QM; DU and QM agree there by construction.**

## 3. Closest existing formalism, and the operational home
The intuition is, almost word-for-word, the **two-state-vector / retrocausal-consistent** reading
(Aharonov's TSVF; Cramer's transactional): the record is fixed by a *global* consistency condition
across the whole causal set. TSVF reproduces QM exactly and is operationally visible in **weak
measurement** (weak values, the three-box paradox) — so weak measurement is where "the record was
already there" becomes operationally sharp, and is the natural regime to instrument.

## 4. The one deviation that could be tested → PRED-DU-001 (SEAM)
An experiment exists only where DU **deviates** from unitary QM. DU ties finality to **irreversible
record accretion / redundancy**, so — *if* finality is a genuine sharp threshold — a
**controlled-redundancy** which-path experiment (tune the number of environmental copies `R`; measure
visibility `V(R)` and recoverability) should show a **knee at `R_c`** and **un-recoverability beyond
the unitary cost**, where standard decoherence predicts a smooth, always-recoverable crossover. This
lives in the **reversible-measurement / Wigner's-friend** regime (trapped-ion / QC), NOT the vanilla
double-slit.

**Status: a conditional SEAM, not a live prediction.** It is a deviation from unitary QM (a large
claim); DU-as-built does not predict it (μ is Born-blind; the count is one-shot); it is doubly-gated
on the mode-issuance unlock (see `flip-witness-algebra-requirements-2026-07-21.md`). Registered as
**PRED-DU-001** with the H1–H7 hardening checklist; it becomes a live REGISTERED prediction the
moment the unlock lands, and is not seeded to the factory before SEED-READY.

## Adversaries
Observer-gradient falsifier — beaten (homogeneous). Block-universe — the §2 explanatory reframe is
*absorbed* by it (that is why §2 is an interpretation, not a prediction); only the §4 deviation could
escape it, and only once the finality threshold is genuine. No-signaling — respected (the effect is
in recoverability/coincidence, never the marginal).
