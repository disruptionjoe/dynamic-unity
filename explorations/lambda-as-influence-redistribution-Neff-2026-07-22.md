# Λ as influence-redistribution pressure (Joe's voting/mechanism framing)

## The idea (Joe)
In a voting system the **natural law** is one-person-one-vote (or one-unit-one-vote — one dollar/one
share). But the *actual* distribution of influence across participants is a **curve**, not flat. And
**higher-order dynamics** — information, media, or the choice of **mechanism** (Condorcet, quadratic,
blind, lottery, …) — put **pressure against that curve**, reshaping it. Those mechanism/information
choices are **initial conditions**. **Λ is that pressure** — the deviation of the actual influence-curve
from the natural one, a *second/third-order structural effect*, not a first-order exponent shift.

## Formalization — the influence-curve and its effective count N_eff
- The **influence-curve** = the distribution of effective weights `w_i` across the records/modes
  (how much each degree of freedom actually contributes to the observable). Its scalar summary is the
  **participation ratio / effective count**: `N_eff = (Σ w_i)² / Σ w_i²` (standard in localization
  physics; `N_eff = N` for uniform weights, `N_eff → 1` for a fully concentrated curve).
- **Natural law (one-record-one-vote = uniform weights):** `N_eff = N` → `Λ ~ 1/√N` (exactly the Sorkin
  value — the "flat" allocation).
- **The pressure (higher-order dynamics / correlations / mechanism concentrate influence):** the curve
  is reshaped, `N_eff < N`, so `Λ ~ 1/√N_eff > 1/√N`. **Λ is enhanced/set by the influence
  concentration**, and the *distance from the natural law* is the participation fraction `N_eff / N`.
- **Initial conditions = the mechanism** (Condorcet / lottery / Shapley / the information environment) —
  the rule that maps the base allocation to the actual influence-curve. Different mechanism → different
  curve → different Λ.

**Why this is second/third-order, not first:** Λ here depends on the **shape of the influence
distribution** (its higher moments / concentration), *not* the leading count or exponent. The leading
1/√N is the "one-vote" natural law; Λ lives in how the weights are *redistributed* on top of it. That is
exactly Joe's "pressure against the curve."

## Connections (this is not a lone analogy)
- **Influence function (statistics):** literally "how much each observation moves the estimator" — the
  influence-curve is a named object; Λ becomes a robustness/leverage measure.
- **Participation ratio / N_eff (condensed-matter localization):** the effective number of contributing
  modes; the measurable handle. `Λ ~ 1/√N_eff`.
- **Shapley value / social choice / mechanism design (Arrow, Condorcet, quadratic voting):** Joe's own
  frame — how a mechanism redistributes influence; ties to the session's earlier "unit of account =
  Shapley" thread. The mechanism is the initial condition.
- **R6a (additive vs heavy-tail):** the additive/uniform regime (1/√N, self-averaging = one-vote) vs the
  heavy-tailed/concentrated regime (τ≈3/2 = influence-concentrated). This candidate is the **continuous
  interpolation** between them — the whole curve — not just the two endpoint exponents.

## The measurable test (richer than the crossover version)
`N_eff / N` (the participation fraction) is measurable and is the "distance from the natural law." The
crux — same as every DE-value attempt — is whether the higher-order dynamics **FORCE** a specific
`N_eff` (a specific influence-curve) or whether `N_eff` is a **free dial** (the coefficient wall relocated
to the participation ratio). The richer structure here is the *hope*: an influence-curve has far more
internal constraint (a mechanism forces a specific reweighting) than a single tuned coefficient, so
there is more that could *force* N_eff. Next swing: build the influence-curve dynamics under a concrete
mechanism, compute `N_eff`, and test whether the mechanism forces it or dials it.

## Relation to the running swing
The Λ-as-criticality build+kill in flight tests the **first-order** version (Λ = crossover scale between
